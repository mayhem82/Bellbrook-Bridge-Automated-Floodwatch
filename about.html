Floodwatch — Bellbrook Bridge What this is Floodwatch is a public, real-time bridge safety display for Bellbrook Bridge. It answers one question only: Can I safely cross the bridge right now? It does this by showing: Current river height Calculated clearance between water and bridge deck A clear human-readable status (Safe / Unsafe) No interpretation required. No login. No alerts. No authority needed. Why Floodwatch exists Existing flood information systems provide: River heights Forecasts Warnings Maps What they do not provide is the one piece of information people actually need at the bridge: How much water is between the river and the road deck. That omission forces people to: Guess Rely on rumours Drive out to check Or trust warnings that are not specific to the crossing they are standing at Floodwatch exists to close that gap. What Floodwatch deliberately does NOT do This is intentional, not a limitation. Floodwatch does not: Forecast future conditions Model scenarios Send alerts Accept user input Infer risk Make recommendations beyond the present moment Integrate with vendors, councils, or agencies Why? Because each of those introduces: Delay Liability deflection Opaque logic Or false confidence Floodwatch shows only what is true now, using publicly available measurements and transparent calculation. How it works (at a high level) Uses live river gauge data Applies a fixed, known bridge deck height Calculates clearance in metres Displays the result plainly There is no hidden logic. No adaptive thresholds. No backend authority. Anyone can check the numbers. My journey This system took approximately six months to build. Not because the idea was complex—but because I had to learn: How river data is actually published How to translate raw measurements into a human decision How to build a public web interface from scratch How to avoid introducing unsafe assumptions I am not a council. I am not a vendor. I am not an emergency authority. I am someone who needed this information and realised it was missing. So I built it. Who this is for Floodwatch is for: Locals who cross the bridge Emergency responders who need confirmation, not forecasts Anyone who wants to independently verify conditions It is not a replacement for warnings or emergency services. It is a missing layer between raw data and real decisions. Core principle Floodwatch exists on one principle: Withholding simple, actionable information does not increase safety. Providing it—clearly and without interpretation—does.
