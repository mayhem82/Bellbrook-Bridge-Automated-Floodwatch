Floodwatch — Bellbrook Bridge

A missing layer between raw data and real decisions.

What this is

Floodwatch is a public, real-time bridge safety display for Bellbrook Bridge. It answers one question only: Can I safely cross the bridge right now?

It does this by showing:

  • Current river height: Real-time data from the local gauge.
  • Calculated clearance: The physical gap between water and bridge deck.
  • Human-readable status: A simple Safe / Unsafe indicator.

No interpretation required. No login. No alerts.

Why Floodwatch Exists

Existing flood information systems provide forecasts, warnings, and maps. However, they often omit the one piece of information people actually need at the riverbank: How much water is between the river and the road deck?

This omission forces locals to guess, rely on rumors, or drive out just to check. Floodwatch exists to close that gap by providing a transparent, measurable truth.

Intentional Limits

Floodwatch does not forecast, model scenarios, or send alerts. This is a deliberate design choice. By focusing only on what is true now, we remove the delay and opaque logic often found in official warning systems. There is no backend authority—anyone can check the numbers themselves.

The Journey

This system took approximately six months to build. It wasn't the code that was difficult, but the learning: translating raw gauge measurements into a human decision and ensuring no unsafe assumptions were introduced.

I am not a council, a vendor, or an emergency authority. I am a resident who needed this information, realized it was missing, and decided to build it.

Core Principle: Withholding simple, actionable information does not increase safety. Providing it—clearly and without interpretation—does.
